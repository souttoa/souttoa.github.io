title: "LastLeg: Replacing Physical Maps with Low-Friction Directions"
---

For the past year, I spent a lot of time thinking about how we navigate unfamiliar large public spaces like shopping centers, concert venues, and university campuses. Physical map kiosks sit scattered around these locations, but people rely heavily on their phones for directions. Standing in front of a giant directory trying to sort through shops and trace a path feels outdated and frustrating.

Apple tried to address this gap by introducing their Indoor Maps program in 2019. The goal was to bring indoor navigation directly to iPhones. The process for venues to join is incredibly strict. Vendors face high barriers to entry, which limits the feature to places with massive annual visitorship. Venues also receive very little dedicated support from Apple once they enroll.

I decided to build a more dedicated alternative. To avoid processing graph theory operations locally, the application sends user coordinates to a GraphHopper server via a REST API. The server computes the shortest path across OpenStreetMap nodes I mapped and returns the exact routing data for MapKit to draw. I paired this external logic with a custom layout engine built in SwiftUI. Moving away from standard Apple view constraints and using absolute coordinate tracking allowed me to build the exact interface I envisioned.

A major focus of the design was making it low-friction and ADA-friendly. Instead of a standard application you have to search for and download, LastLeg runs as an iOS App Clip. You simply scan a code that tells the app your location, and it provides personalized directions. The app includes features to meet ADA compliance measures by prioritizing routes with ramps and elevators to make moving around easier for people with disabilities.

The current version works well outdoors and in simple indoor areas. I plan to improve the precise indoor tracking next. I want to integrate inexpensive iBeacon units to pinpoint a user's location indoors where GPS signals drop. Applying this hardware will make turn-by-turn indoor directions highly accurate.
